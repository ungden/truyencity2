## 1. Rubric: 10 Quality Dimensions

Each dimension scored 1-10. Reference đại thần authors for context:
- **10**: Peak đại thần (Ngã Cật Tây Hồng Thị at his best, Thần Trư's signature arcs)
- **8-9**: Published đại thần quality, would retain 80%+ readers
- **6-7**: Competent webnovel, readable but forgettable
- **5**: Generic AI / mediocre human writer
- **3-4**: Noticeably AI-generated, repetitive
- **1-2**: Unreadable

| # | Dimension | What It Measures | Đại Thần Benchmark |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| D1 | **Prose Craft** | Sentence variety, sensory detail, metaphor quality, avoiding cliché | Thần Trư: every metaphor earns its place; no wasted adjectives |
| D2 | **Character Voice** | Distinct speech patterns per character, personality through dialogue | Ngã Cật: MC's shameless voice vs villain's cold politeness are unmistakable |
| D3 | **Emotional Depth** | Inner monologue layers, emotional contrast, reader empathy | Three-layer inner thoughts: surface calm → deeper anxiety → deepest despair |
| D4 | **Plot Architecture** | Setup/payoff within chapter, cliffhanger quality, arc progression | Each chapter advances 2-3 plot threads and plants 1+ seeds for future |
| D5 | **Worldbuilding Integration** | Rules feel organic (not info-dump), consistent power system, geography | Power system rules emerge through combat, not exposition paragraphs |
| D6 | **Pacing & Rhythm** | Scene length variation, tension curves, breather moments | Fast→slow→fast rhythm; action chapters have 1 quiet moment for contrast |
| D7 | **Comedy & Subtext** | Humor through character (not random jokes), dialogue with hidden meanings | Gap Moe, Não Bổ, villain speaks politely while planning murder |
| D8 | **Anti-AI Naturalness** | Avoids banned phrases, no repetitive patterns, no "AI voice" | Human reader cannot distinguish from human-written webnovel |
| D9 | **Dopamine Engineering** | Face-slap payoff, power reveals, addiction hooks per engagement checklist | Reader physically cannot stop reading — every chapter ends with "one more" |
| D10 | **Continuity & Logic** | No contradictions with previous chapters, consistent character behavior | Zero plot holes, power levels never exceed established limits |

## 4. Top 5 Gaps (Ranked by Impact × Fixability)

### GAP 1: Comedy is Completely Absent (Score: 1.3/6.0, Gap: -4.7)

**Impact**: CRITICAL. Comedy is what makes Chinese webnovels addictive. Even the darkest Thần Trư arcs have MC's inner monologue providing dry humor. The COMEDY_MECHANICS_RULES exist in templates.ts but are clearly not being utilized by the Writer agent.

**Root Cause**: 
- `chapter-writer.ts` WRITER_SYSTEM prompt mentions "SẢN XUẤT SƯỚNG VĂN" but doesn't explicitly require comedy beats
- The Architect outline (`SceneOutline`) has no comedy/humor field
- The Critic doesn't score or enforce humor
- During high-intensity arcs, the model naturally suppresses humor because it "feels wrong" — but đại thần authors know comedy during tension is precisely what makes the tension bearable

**Fix**:
1. Add `comedyBeat?: string` to `SceneOutline` interface
2. Add to ARCHITECT_SYSTEM: "Mỗi chương PHẢI có ít nhất 1 khoảnh khắc hài hước (Não Bổ, Vô Sỉ, Phản Kém, hoặc Hệ Thống Troll). Ngay cả trong trận chiến sinh tử, MC phải có ít nhất 1 suy nghĩ nội tâm tự giễu nhại hoặc bình luận khô khan."
3. Add `comedy_score` to Critic scoring (min 4 to APPROVE)

### GAP 2: Pacing is Stuck at Maximum Intensity (Score: 5.5/8.0, Gap: -2.5)

**Impact**: HIGH. Reader fatigue. When everything is at 10/10 intensity, nothing feels intense. Đại thần authors use the "peaks and valleys" technique — a devastating battle is followed by MC eating a bowl of noodles in silence. This contrast is what creates emotional resonance.

**Root Cause**:
- `pacing-director.ts` generates `arc_pacing_blueprints` with mood types, but the Writer prompt doesn't enforce mandatory "valley" scenes
- The `SCENE_EXPANSION_RULES` specify per-scene pacing (action: fast sentences, emotional: long sentences) but there's no rule requiring scene TYPE variety within a chapter
- Architect outline allows all scenes to be the same type (all action)

**Fix**:
1. Add to ARCHITECT_SYSTEM: "Mỗi chương có 4-6 scenes PHẢI bao gồm ít nhất 1 scene nhịp chậm (đối thoại, suy nghĩ nội tâm, ký ức, hoặc slice-of-life). Cấm toàn bộ scenes đều là chiến đấu/hành động."
2. Add `sceneTypeVariety` check to Critic: reject if all scenes are same type
3. In `pacing-director.ts`, add per-chapter mood target that alternates (not just per-arc)

### GAP 3: Severe Word/Color Repetition — AI Fingerprint (Score: 5.0/8.0, Gap: -3.0)

**Impact**: HIGH. This is the #1 thing that makes the text feel AI-generated. Specific issues:
- "tím sẫm/tím đen" appears 20+ times across 4 chapters
- "vàng kim" appears 25+ times
- "pixel hóa" appears 10+ times  
- "rực rỡ" appears 8+ times
- "kinh hoàng" appears 8+ times
- "ken két" appears 4 times
- "rỉ sét" appears 15+ times (in Ch162-163)

**Root Cause**:
- `ANTI_CLICHE_RULES` bans specific phrases but does NOT address color/adjective repetition
- `voice-fingerprint.ts` tracks style drift but doesn't track word-level repetition
- No post-processing step to detect and flag overused words
- The Writer model naturally latches onto "signature" colors (tím for MC, vàng kim for enemy) and overuses them

**Fix**:
1. Add to `ANTI_CLICHE_RULES`: "KHÔNG được sử dụng cùng một tính từ màu sắc quá 3 lần trong một chương. Phải dùng từ đồng nghĩa hoặc miêu tả gián tiếp. Ví dụ: thay vì 'tím sẫm' lần thứ 4, dùng 'màu của bầm tím trên da', 'sắc hoàng hôn pha loãng', 'như mực cũ loang trên giấy'."
2. Add to CRITIC_SYSTEM: "Kiểm tra lặp từ: Nếu bất kỳ tính từ/trạng từ nào xuất hiện >4 lần → REVISE. Nếu >6 lần → REWRITE."
3. Add post-write word frequency check in orchestrator (pure JS, no AI call needed) that warns when repetition is detected

### GAP 4: Characters Sound the Same (Score: 5.3/8.0, Gap: -2.7)

**Impact**: HIGH. In đại thần novels, you can identify who's speaking without dialogue tags. In our output, if you remove the names, Trần Vũ, Aria, Số 01, and Người Giám Sát are interchangeable.

**Root Cause**:
- `character-arc-engine.ts` tracks `signature_traits` and `speech_patterns` but this data is injected as context, not as hard constraints
- The Writer prompt says "giọng riêng mỗi nhân vật" but doesn't provide concrete examples
- The `character_states` table has bad data (names like '001') which means character voice profiles are often empty

**Fix**:
1. For each major character, define 3 concrete speech rules in the character arc data:
   - Trần Vũ: Uses profanity when stressed, speaks in short declarative sentences, occasionally self-deprecating
   - Aria-Zero: Technical jargon + sarcastic commentary, addresses MC as "Ngài" but drops formality when panicked
   - Lâm Tịch: Sparse dialogue, poetic phrasing, uses "anh trai" 
   - Villains: Never use crude language, speak in metaphors (per SUBTEXT_DIALOGUE_RULES)
2. Add `characterVoiceGuide` as a REQUIRED field in Writer prompt (not optional context)
3. Fix character name extraction (the '001' bug) to ensure voice profiles match real characters

### GAP 5: Emotional Depth is Surface-Level (Score: 6.0/8.0, Gap: -2.0)

**Impact**: MEDIUM-HIGH. The SCENE_EXPANSION_RULES specify "layered inner monologue (surface → deeper → deepest)" but the actual output only does 1 layer. Example: "Trần Vũ cảm thấy đầu óc mình quay cuồng. Những ký ức đau khổ nhất..." — this tells us he's suffering but doesn't make us feel it.

**Root Cause**:
- Inner monologue rules exist but are buried in SCENE_EXPANSION_RULES
- The Writer model compresses inner thoughts to save token budget for action
- No Critic check for inner monologue depth

**Fix**:
1. Add to WRITER_SYSTEM: "Mỗi scene chính PHẢI có ít nhất 1 đoạn nội tâm 3 lớp: (Bề mặt) MC nghĩ gì → (Sâu hơn) MC thực sự cảm thấy gì → (Sâu nhất) Nỗi sợ/mong muốn mà MC không dám thừa nhận."
2. Add Critic check: "Nếu chapter không có ít nhất 2 đoạn nội tâm đa lớp → REVISE"
