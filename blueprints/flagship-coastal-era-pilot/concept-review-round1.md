# Flagship coastal-era pilot — blind concept review round 1

Project: `3434aec5-04f6-483a-bfa3-a84bef2532ea`

This packet hides model routes and automated ranking. No concept has been selected or materialized.

## Candidate A

Core loop: read tidal flow and crustacean behavior → build low-cost traps → harvest → sell → buy food and medicine.

Opening progression:

1. The protagonist recognizes that losing the family boat does not remove access to the tidal creek and builds three bamboo traps.
2. He exchanges labor for discarded fish bait and places the traps in mangrove roots using remembered current patterns.
3. He earns cash from a large mud crab, buys rice and medicine, and brings home the first full meal.

Strengths:

- Best cause-and-effect chain and most believable starting capital: scrap bamboo, labor, bait waste and local knowledge.
- Agency appears immediately; chapter 3 pays both material and family comfort promises.
- The advantage is useful without giving exact prices, treasure coordinates or free equipment.
- Can expand into traps, species knowledge, habitat stewardship and a small harvesting group.

Risks to repair in StorySpec:

- The current conflict engine leans on curious or jealous villagers; replace generic envy with concrete competition for trap locations, bait, access paths and sustainable catch limits.
- Do not treat mangrove resources as unlimited or imply that every trap produces rare catch.
- Verify species, trap design, tides and local preservation before prose generation.

## Candidate B

Core loop: buy low-value mixed catch → sort and salt correctly → dry cleanly → negotiate a higher-value product.

Opening progression:

1. The protagonist spends the household's final food money on mixed low-value catch.
2. The family sorts, salts and builds an elevated drying rack.
3. A wholesaler buys the finished product and the family gets a meat meal.

Strengths:

- Strongest post-harvest value-add mechanism and good roles for the mother and wife.
- Physical work, spoilage risk and processing steps create visible competence scenes.
- Naturally supports later product quality, packaging and market-access arcs.

Critical risks:

- The opening claims roughly 5,000 becomes 105,000 in one batch; this is not credible without sourced quantities, yields and period prices.
- Spending the final household cash plus ridicule/tearful resistance pushes the tone closer to manufactured suffering.
- The wholesaler scene risks becoming a stock face-slap loop and the technique is framed too much like a secret monopoly.

Verdict: do not select without rebuilding the opening economics.

## Candidate C

Core loop: exploit the coastal/inland fresh-fish price gap → preserve with ice and insulation → bicycle to market → reinvest in carrying capacity.

Opening progression:

1. The protagonist uses the household's last rice money to buy fish and ice.
2. He cycles twenty kilometers and sells directly at an inland market.
3. He buys meat and larger baskets, then faces limited ice supply.

Strengths:

- Fastest pleasure cadence and clearest visible progression from cash to tools.
- Transport time, temperature and carrying capacity can generate varied operational scenes.
- The family payoff lands cleanly in chapter 3.

Critical risks:

- The implied purchase and resale multiples are not yet credible for 1988 and need sourced units/prices.
- The insulation claim needs technical verification under the stated distance and heat.
- The next conflict already drifts toward an ice monopoly and hostile traders, which can become artificial escalation.

Verdict: viable only after domain and price reconstruction; weaker than A for the first pilot.

## Recommendation

Select **Candidate A** conditionally. It best satisfies pleasure-first without relying on implausible capital multiplication. Before StorySpec approval, replace generic village jealousy with resource competition by actors who have legitimate livelihoods, and bind the 21–30 chapter arc to sustainable harvesting rather than endless catch growth.

Candidate B is the strongest backup mechanism after a full unit-economics rewrite. Candidate C should remain a later market/transport story, not the opening flagship.
